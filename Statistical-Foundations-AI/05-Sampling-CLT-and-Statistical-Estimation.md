# 05 - Sampling, CLT, and Statistical Estimation

## 5.1 Sampling Methods
- Simple random sampling
- Stratified sampling
- Cluster sampling
- Convenience sampling (high risk of bias)

## 5.2 Law of Large Numbers
Sample averages converge toward population mean as sample size grows.

## 5.3 Central Limit Theorem
Under broad conditions, sample mean distribution approaches normal for large n.

## 5.4 Standard Error
Quantifies uncertainty in sample estimate.

## 5.5 Point Estimation Properties
- Unbiasedness
- Consistency
- Efficiency

## 5.6 Bias-Variance Tradeoff
Lower bias often increases variance and vice versa. Good estimators balance both.

## 5.7 Real-Time Example
You estimate model accuracy from 500 sampled predictions.
CLT enables an approximate confidence interval around measured accuracy.
