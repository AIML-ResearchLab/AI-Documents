# 12 - Causal Inference and Counterfactual Thinking

## 12.1 Correlation Is Not Causation
Observed association can be driven by confounders.

## 12.2 Counterfactual Question
What would have happened to same unit without treatment?

## 12.3 Confounding and Bias
- Omitted variables
- Selection bias
- Survivorship bias

## 12.4 DAG Intuition
Directed acyclic graphs help identify adjustment sets and blocked paths.

## 12.5 Causal Strategies (Intro)
- Randomized experiments
- Matching and stratification
- Difference-in-differences intuition
- Instrumental variable intuition

## 12.6 Simpson’s Paradox
Global trend may reverse within subgroups.
Always inspect stratified analysis.

## 12.7 AI Example
Ad campaign appears to improve conversion overall, but subgroup analysis shows effect driven by traffic source mix shift.
